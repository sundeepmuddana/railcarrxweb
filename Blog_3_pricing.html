How Repair Shops can Capture more Revenue by Changing Pricing Approach

Is your repair shop losing bids to gain new customers? Perhaps your approach to capturing new business needs some tweaking.

Repair shops are constantly looking for ways to win more business from equipment owners. Equipment owners evaluate repair shops on several factors when deciding to enter an agreement with them. Quality of work, capacity, and cost are few major factors in this decision. RailcarRx Repair Shop helps to manage capacity planning and definitely can help when it comes to managing customer specific labor rates.

At RailcarRx we believe in using successful ideas from other industries and applying them to rail. For example, when I think back to any contract I have signed for a service, I realize that I chose that provider based on the best rates (when services are comparable). To “sweeten the pot” the service provider may offer a multi-year contract with introductory rates for the first year, then each subsequent year the rates increase per the agreement. Think cable or internet providers here, for example, my internet provider charges me an introductory rate of $35 per month year one, then year two and three the rate increases to $45 per month.

RailcarRx thought “why not apply this same methodology to railcar repairs?” Most repair shops that are performing contract repairs provide car owners or maintenance responsible parties with a negotiated rate for labor, material markups, etc. Shops may be able to win more customers by providing an introductory labor rate of $75 per hour for year one, then the rates increase years 2 & 3 for example.

Some shops are already offering this type of pricing model, however, the majority of off-the-shelf shop systems do not provide functionality to effectively manage when and how rates expire and new rates are applied. These systems force users to manually update rates after they have expired. As we know, adding any manual manipulation will almost always be a point of failure. Meaning that customers may continue to enjoy the same introductory rates you provided to win their business for the life of the agreement. Good for the car owners, not so good for the shop.

RailcarRx Repair Shop takes this concept and applies it to repair rates using the tiered pricing feature in our solution. This provides repair shops the flexibility to get more creative with pricing and material markups to help attract more customers. RailcarRx Repair Shop allow shops to have an unlimited number of pricing tiers that automatically expire when a new tier’s effective date is reached. No manual updates required, no human error.

In the screen shot below, from the RailcarRx Repair Shop solution, a shop can set effective dates for each pricing tier, applicable labor rates, material markups, and other rate types as well. The area highlighted within the red box shows the effective date of each pricing tier, the standard labor and tank car rates, and material markup rate. As each effective date passes, the previous pricing tier expires.

As consumer spending and confidence continues to grow, both the economy and the rail industry will share in the success from this renewed forward momentum.

RailcarRx Repair Shop offers users the ability to setup tiered pricing for customers

In this current business environment repair shops need to market their services and rates differently than their competitors. RailcarRx Repair Shop provides the edge for a shop to easily offer and manage a tiered pricing structure that automatically manages itself. How could your shop benefit from this ability?
